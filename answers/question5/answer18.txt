The French Revolution and the Napoleonic Wars were significant events in European history, impacting politics, society, and warfare. The French Revolution began in 1789, overthrowing the monarchy and leading to the rise of Napoleon Bonaparte. Napoleon's military campaigns, known as the Napoleonic Wars, reshaped Europe's political landscape through conquests and alliances. Battles like Austerlitz and Waterloo were pivotal in determining the outcome of these conflicts. The Napoleonic era ended with Napoleon's defeat and exile to Saint Helena. Overall, these events marked a period of profound change and conflict in European history.
