India's journey since the Green Revolution has been marked by significant economic and social reforms. These reforms tackled challenges like poverty and aimed to propel the nation towards development.

A major economic shift came in 1991 under Prime Minister P.V. Narasimha Rao and Finance Minister Dr. Manmohan Singh.  This period, known as liberalization, moved India away from a socialist model towards a market-oriented approach. This meant reducing trade barriers, cutting red tape, and welcoming foreign investment. The results were impressive, with increased GDP growth, stronger global ties, and a rise in living standards for many Indians.

The agricultural sector, despite the Green Revolution's initial success, had issues. Various policies addressed these. The National Agricultural Policy (2000) aimed for sustainable practices, better rural lives, and food security. Initiatives like the Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY) focused on boosting productivity, supporting farmers, and modernizing agriculture. 

Healthcare and education also saw reforms. The National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) of 2005 aimed to provide accessible and affordable quality healthcare in rural areas. It focused on strengthening infrastructure, training healthcare workers, and prioritizing maternal and child health. Similarly, the Right to Education Act (RTE) of 2009 mandated free and compulsory education for all children aged 6 to 14. 

India has also undertaken urban development initiatives. The Jawaharlal Nehru National Urban Renewal Mission (JNNURM) launched in 2005 aimed to improve urban infrastructure, governance, and basic services for residents. It focused on areas like housing, water supply, sanitation, and transportation to create sustainable and inclusive cities. From economic liberalization and agricultural improvements to social welfare programs and urban development initiatives, the country has made significant strides in improving the lives of its citizens and fostering development.
