The Mauryan Empire, founded by Chandragupta Maurya, expanded under subsequent rulers like Bindusara and reached its zenith under Ashoka the Great.
Chandragupta's conquest of Magadha marked the empire's establishment, followed by Bindusara's expansion southward into the Deccan plateau.
Ashoka's transformative Kalinga campaign led to his conversion to Buddhism and ethical governance.
The empire's military conquests secured trade routes and facilitated cultural exchanges with the Hellenistic world.
The centralized administrative system ensured stability and prosperity across Mauryan territories, spanning from Magadha to modern-day Pakistan, Afghanistan, and the Deccan plateau.
Ashoka's edicts advocated for righteousness and welfare, leaving a lasting legacy of compassion and enlightenment.
